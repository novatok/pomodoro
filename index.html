The Pomodoro Technique

"Pomodoro is the italian word for tomato."

If you have open-ended work, like studying or coding, that could take unlimited amounts of time or even if you get distracted easily and spend hours mindlessly browsing tiktok, then the pomodoro technique could be just for you.

Developed by Francesco Cirillo in the late 1980s, pomodoro technique is a time management system that uses a kitchen timer to break work into intervals, typically 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks.

This timer follows the pomodoro method giving you 25 minutes for deep focus and a short break of 5 minutes. An alarm will sound at the end of each time period. You can pause, play and reset the timer as needed.
